About: Research Proposal

This document proposes research into Gamification and its significance within the context of providing a solution to improving the quality and quantity of responses in conducting a survey. Surveys are widely known as a method in which researchers gather information from a sample of participants. Surveys afford researchers access to the knowledge or information economy of societies. Over time, before the mechanical reproduction of information through technology. Surveys were conducted face-to-face and telephone surveys.

However, during the past decades since the proliferation of technology surveys have been online. Although, due to the vast nature of the medium participants often leave surveys uncompleted in search of something “enjoyable” and “fun” online. Therefore, I aim to use game elements, and design principles to attempt to provide impetus and a framework within which people could willingly participate in a survey that is “enjoyable”, “fun”, and meaningful. This will define the practical purpose of creating a gamified product as a means of practice as research.

The theoretical component of my research will focus on examining how the MDA framework coupled with literature on defining, researching gamification can be used to design a gamified survey? Therefore, most of the reasoning and qualitative data will predominately refer to the practical component and its significance through the data collected, with regards to its quantity and quality. Hence the significance of the project largely aims to gamify surveys as a means for collecting greater and substantive data by engaging users through a ‘gameful experience.
